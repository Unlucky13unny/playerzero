FAQs

What is the PlayerZero Challenge?

A competitive week-long event where Pokémon GO players flex their grind. It's not affiliated with Niantic or any publisher — just community-fueled fun with real rewards.

How do I join?

Submit your Trainer Code, stats, and screenshots through the entry form and pay the entry fee to lock in your spot.

When does the challenge start?

The event officially begins after the minimum entry threshold is reached — but the exact start date will be announced in advance so players have time to prep and recruit.

What stats count?

This challenge is all about measurable grind — XP gained, Pokémon caught, distance walked, PokéStops visited, and more. Scoring may focus on a single category or combine multiple metrics, depending on participation. Final rules will be announced before launch.

What do I win?

Cash prizes for the top performers, bonus rewards for social challenge winners, and increasing prize tiers as more players join.

How are winners notified?

We’ll email winners using the contact address they submit. For updates and community interaction, follow @pgPlayerZero on Instagram.

What prevents cheating?

Submissions are manually verified using image readers and stat matching. Screenshots must match your stats, and obvious manipulation will result in disqualification without refund.

Is player level a scoring category?

No. All scoring metrics are performance-based — everyone, from new players to veterans, competes on equal footing.

What if I miss the start?

You can still join as long as the challenge is active. Submit your entry and screenshots before the deadline to have your progress counted for the week.

What does the entry fee go toward?

100% goes into supporting infrastructure and building the prize pool. The more participants, the more categories — and the more people get paid.

Can I get a refund?

No refunds are available. The event uses pooled prize tiers that expand with more players, so each entry directly supports the payout scale.
